The [schedule][] for [#HarvardDataFest][] is jam packed with awesome talks.

[schedule]: https://harvarddatafest2020.sched.com/
[#HarvardDataFest]: https://twitter.com/hashtag/HarvardDataFest

In [Data Visualization with Tableau][] with Jess Cohen-Tanugi we looked at [storms data][]. She showed how to create maps and blend data sources. Afterward, we got an amazing demo of [Glue][].

[Data Visualization with Tableau]: https://harvarddatafest2020.sched.com/event/YQFS/data-visualization-with-tableau
[storms data]: http://bit.ly/storms_data
[Glue]: http://glueviz.org/

[Reproducible & Collaborative Data Science with the Renku Platform][] with [Rok Roskar][]. The Renku [first steps][] docs show integration with Dataverse and I couldn't resist [tweeting][] about it.

[Reproducible & Collaborative Data Science with the Renku Platform]: https://harvarddatafest2020.sched.com/event/YNiL/reproducible-collaborative-data-science-with-the-renku-platform
[Rok Roskar]: https://twitter.com/rokstars
[first steps]: https://renku.readthedocs.io/en/latest/user/firststeps.html
[tweeting]: https://twitter.com/philipdurbin/status/1219701018474119168

[Interactive Data Visualization with Shiny in R][] with Ista Zahn and Jess Cohen-Tanugi. Ista's [shiny_workshop][] GitHub repo has the full tutorial.

[Interactive Data Visualization with Shiny in R]: https://harvarddatafest2020.sched.com/event/YNj4/interactive-data-visualization-with-shiny-in-r
[shiny_workshop]: https://github.com/izahn/shiny_workshop
