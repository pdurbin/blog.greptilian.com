My daughter was introduced to [Desmos][] in her 8th grade math class and she's having a blast creating art with it.

[Desmos]: https://www.desmos.com

![](/images/desmos-logo.svg)

The [Wikipedia article][] for Desmos describes it as an **advanced graphing calculator** and mentions toward the end that "another peculiar use of the calculator involves the creation of graphic arts."

[Wikipedia article]: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desmos_(graphing)

Sure enough, the [Staff Picks: Creative Art][] link on the Desmos home page reveals pictures of [bears][], a [shark][], a [chick][], a [seal][], and more. I find the bears especially cute.

![](/images/desmos-bears.svg)

[Staff Picks: Creative Art]: https://www.desmos.com/art
[bears]: https://www.desmos.com/calculator/syweivhdj1
[shark]: https://www.desmos.com/calculator/swbbunviaw
[chick]: https://www.desmos.com/calculator/erbfzwwm9t
[seal]: https://www.desmos.com/calculator/9aie97zc5x

But how do you get started? I clicked "Start Graphing" and asked my daughter what to type. She started me off with two equations:

```
y = 2x + 8
x^2 + y^2 = 8
```
Suddently, I had created my [first][] graph with Desmos, which looks like this:
